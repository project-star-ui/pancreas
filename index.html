What is the Pancreas?

Your pancreas is a special helper in your body, shaped like a fish and hiding behind your stomach. It's about the size of your hand when you make a fist!

Even though you can't see it or feel it working, your pancreas is always busy helping you digest your food and keep your body healthy.

Did You Know?

The pancreas is both a chef and a mail carrier! It makes special juices to help digest food (chef) and sends important messages to control your blood sugar (mail carrier).

How the Pancreas Helps Your Tummy

When you eat yummy food like pizza or fruit, here's what your pancreas does:

  1. The pancreas makes special digestive "juices" full of tiny helpers called enzymes.
  2. These juices travel through a tiny tube into your small intestine.
  3. The enzymes break down food into super small pieces so your body can use all the good stuff.
  4. Carbohydrates (like bread) get broken into sugar for energy.
  5. Proteins (like chicken) get broken into building blocks for your muscles.
  6. Fats (like butter) get broken down so your body can use them too.

The Pancreas Superpower

The pancreas has another important job - it helps control your blood sugar!

It makes insulin, which is like a key that lets sugar from your food enter your body's cells to give you energy. Without insulin, the sugar would just stay in your blood.

Body Fact:

The pancreas makes just the right amount of insulin you need - not too much and not too little! It's like having a perfect sugar helper inside you.

How to Keep Your Pancreas Happy

Here are some ways to take care of your pancreas:

  • Eat colorful fruits and vegetables
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Get exercise every day
  • Don't eat too much sugar
  • Get enough sleep

Remember, your pancreas works hard every day to keep you healthy, so it's important to treat it well!
